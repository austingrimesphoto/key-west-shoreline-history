    How dates enter the timeline

    A date is selectable only when it has unique spatial evidence.

    The application queries NOAA’s live historical-aerial catalog over the Key West study bounds, groups returned catalog rasters by their actual year, and creates one locked raster mosaic per verified year. It never relabels the 1907 chart as another date.

    1. Fixed chart: the 1907 georeferenced Admiralty chart has its own tile source and appears once.
    2. Discovered aerial year: NOAA must return at least one exact-year catalog raster intersecting Key West before that year appears.
    3. Modern reference: NOAA’s live shoreline service represents the present-day comparison state.
    4. Milestone only: historically important dates stay in the narrative until a defensible spatial layer is acquired.

    NOAA describes the historical aerial photographs as georeferenced but not orthorectified. They are useful visual evidence, but alignment error is possible and should not be interpreted as survey-grade precision.

    Survey-coverage rectangles remain metadata envelopes, not coastlines. No historical land polygon is fabricated to make the timeline look complete.

    Detailed methods and status are in docs/DATA_METHOD.md and docs/IMPLEMENTATION_STATUS.md.

    About this project

    This public historical GIS reconstructs the physical growth of Key West, with special attention to Trumbo Point, the Florida East Coast Railway, wartime naval development, and later reclamation.

    Current map rule: the timeline includes only dates backed by a distinct spatial layer. NOAA historical-aerial years are discovered and verified at runtime; unsupported milestone dates remain visible in the narrative but cannot be selected as maps.

    Basemap: OpenFreeMap and OpenStreetMap contributors. Each historical source retains its own credit and rights statement.

    What the map currently proves

    Visible survey rectangles are coverage—not coastline.

    They are constructed directly from NOAA’s published metadata extents and dates. They show where authoritative shoreline data exists while the vector downloads are being acquired.

    1. Surveyed: NOAA vector shoreline or a Coast Survey manuscript with documented control.
    2. Aerial-derived: traced from a dated, georeferenced aerial image.
    3. Mapped: traced from a georeferenced historical map.
    4. Approximate: incomplete evidence, visibly styled and explained as uncertain.

    The 1907 raster is a genuine georeferenced chart overlay, but its five control points make it interpretation-grade. No historical land polygon has been fabricated to make the timeline appear complete.

    Detailed methods and status are in docs/DATA_METHOD.md and docs/IMPLEMENTATION_STATUS.md.

    About this project

    This public historical GIS reconstructs the physical growth of Key West, with special attention to Trumbo Point, the Florida East Coast Railway, wartime naval development, and later reclamation.

    Current working release: source-backed timeline, real NOAA survey footprints, a live NOAA modern shoreline request, and a georeferenced 1907 chart overlay. Historical shoreline polygons will appear only after the underlying vector data is imported and reviewed.

    Basemap: OpenFreeMap and OpenStreetMap contributors. Each historical source retains its own credit and rights statement.

    Source catalog

    Every selectable date resolves to its own source. Approximate historical-image fits are labeled separately from controlled charts and NOAA data.

    How the overlays should be interpreted

    Approximate is useful, provided it is visible and honest.

    Regional colonial maps are fitted to broad coastlines and can be manually adjusted. Distortion varies across each sheet, so agreement near Cuba may worsen agreement near Florida. These layers are for exploration, names, and geographic context—not shoreline measurement.

    1. Experimental image fit: a public-domain scan placed with a best-effort rectangular transformation and user-adjustable controls.
    2. Controlled chart: the 1907 Admiralty chart uses a published georeferenced tile source.
    3. NOAA aerial: optional exact-year rasters, added only when the same-origin catalog proxy returns them.
    4. Modern reference: NOAA’s current shoreline comparison, loaded independently.

    NOAA discovery has a seven-second limit and must end in loaded, empty, or unavailable status. It cannot block the locally stored maps.

    Detailed methods and status are in docs/DATA_METHOD.md and docs/IMPLEMENTATION_STATUS.md.

    About this project

    This public historical GIS reconstructs the physical growth of Key West, with special attention to Trumbo Point, the Florida East Coast Railway, naval development, and reclamation.

    Current release: fixed historical maps load without NOAA. Colonial and Coast Survey sheets can be adjusted manually; their experimental alignment is clearly distinguished from controlled evidence.

    Basemap: OpenFreeMap and OpenStreetMap contributors. Each historical source retains its own credit and rights statement.
